I live and work in Missoula, Montana. I originally came here to study Evolutionary Biology and Genomics at the University of Montana and quickly fell in love with this part of the country. My MS research focused on understanding the evolution and regulation of seasonal coat color change in dwarf hamsters. While working with genomic data, I developed a love for coding and thinking about complex sets of data. Now, I work as a data analyst for a community health center that focuses on patient-centered comprehensive primary care. My days are often occupied by querying our electronic medical record database, wrangling and analyzing datasets, and visually communicating patterns and insights gleaned from these data. I always strive to communicate data with an aesthetic approach and to inspire the viewer. I graduated with a bachelors in Ecology and Native American Studies from Dartmouth College.

In my free time, I enjoy exploring the area where I live rock climbing, mountain biking, and skiing. You can also find me at a live music performance or preparing a new recipe in my kitchen.
